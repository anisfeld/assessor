Cook County Market Value Ratios 2009-2015 for Single-family Homes and Condos

Cook County Assessor Joe Berrios's job is to estimate the value of property in the county so that taxes can be levied in proportion to property values. Using data from 2009 to 2015 (2015 is the latest year it is available), we checked to see whether he did his job well.

The results are in: Berrios produced poor quality, unfair assessments. Berrios's bad assessments shifted taxes away from high value properties and onto lower value properties. In effect, Berrios made poorer residents in Cook County pay part of the tax bills of richer residents in Cook County. In many cases, taxpayers paid hundreds of dollars more in property taxes than they would have had Berrios done his job well.

This map confirms what The Chicago Tribune , ProPublica, and The University of Chicago have already told us - Joe Berrios produce bad and unfair assessments. Here, we map the ratio of Berrios's estimate of home values to the sale price of homes, in the year they sold. A ratio of 1 shows that the Berrios's estimate was exactly equal to the sale price of the home. A ratio greater than 1 shows that Berrios over-estimated the value of a home, and therefore the home paid more taxes than they should have. Conversely, a ratio less than 1 shows that Berrios under-estimated properties' values, leading to lower tax bills for those properties. It is important to remember that under-value properties are not simply taxed less, they shift their unpaid amounts to the over-valued properties.

In the map, lighter colors have lower market value ratios. Clearly, Berrios consistently valued north and central Chicago properties lower than western and southern properties. This produced unfair shifting of taxes from high-value, mostly white areas of the county to lower-value, mostly minority areas of the county. Joe Berrios has clearly failed to perform the central duties of his office. His failures as an assessor have unnecessarily and unfairly increased taxes for minority and low-income residents in this County.

Cook County deserves a competent, fair assessor.
